PREAMBLE — THE OBJECTIVES RESOLUTION
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Whereas sovereignty over the entire Universe belongs to Almighty Allah alone,
and the authority to be exercised by the people of Pakistan within the limits
prescribed by Him is a sacred trust;

And whereas it is the will of the people of Pakistan to establish an order:
  - Wherein the State shall exercise its powers through chosen representatives;
  - Wherein the principles of democracy, freedom, equality, tolerance and social
    justice, as enunciated by Islam, shall be fully observed;
  - Wherein Muslims shall be enabled to order their lives in accordance with the
    teachings of the Holy Quran and Sunnah;
  - Wherein adequate provision shall be made for minorities to freely profess
    and practice their religions;
  - Wherein the territories now included in or in accession with Pakistan and
    such other territories as may hereafter be included or accede to Pakistan
    shall form a Federation;

[Note: The Objectives Resolution was made a substantive part of the Constitution
by Article 2-A, inserted by the Revival of Constitution of 1973 Order, 1985.]

PART I — INTRODUCTORY (Articles 1–6)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 1 — THE REPUBLIC AND ITS TERRITORIES
  Pakistan shall be a Federal Republic known as the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.
  Territories of Pakistan comprise:
    (a) Provinces of Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Punjab, and Sindh;
    (b) Islamabad Capital Territory (Federal Capital);
    (c) Gilgit-Baltistan (as per 25th Amendment mainstreaming FATA, 2018);
    (d) Such states and territories as are or may be included in Pakistan.
  Parliament may by law admit new States or areas into the Federation.

ARTICLE 2 — ISLAM AS STATE RELIGION
  Islam shall be the State religion of Pakistan.

ARTICLE 2-A — OBJECTIVES RESOLUTION AS SUBSTANTIVE PART
  The Objectives Resolution is made a substantive part of the Constitution.

ARTICLE 3 — ELIMINATION OF EXPLOITATION
  The State shall ensure elimination of all forms of exploitation and the
  gradual fulfilment of the fundamental principle: from each according to
  his ability, to each according to his work.

ARTICLE 4 — RIGHT OF INDIVIDUALS TO BE DEALT WITH IN ACCORDANCE WITH LAW
  To enjoy protection of law and to be treated in accordance with law is the
  inalienable right of every citizen, wherever he may be, and of every other
  person for the time being within Pakistan.
    (a) No action detrimental to life, liberty, body, reputation or property
        of any person shall be taken except in accordance with law;
    (b) No person shall be prevented from doing that which is not prohibited by law;
    (c) No person shall be compelled to do that which law does not require.

ARTICLE 5 — LOYALTY TO STATE AND OBEDIENCE TO CONSTITUTION
  (1) Loyalty to the State is the basic duty of every citizen.
  (2) Obedience to the Constitution and law is the inviolable obligation of
      every citizen wherever he may be and of every other person in Pakistan.

ARTICLE 6 — HIGH TREASON
  (1) Any person who abrogates or attempts or conspires to abrogate, subverts
      or attempts or conspires to subvert the Constitution by use of force or
      show of force or by other unconstitutional means shall be guilty of high
      treason.
  (2) Any person aiding or abetting such acts shall also be guilty of high treason.
  (3) Parliament shall by law provide for punishment of persons found guilty
      of high treason.

PART II — FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS (Articles 8–28)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 8 — LAWS INCONSISTENT WITH FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS TO BE VOID
  Any law, or any custom or usage having the force of law, inconsistent with
  the rights conferred by this Chapter shall, to the extent of such
  inconsistency, be void. The State shall not make any such law.

ARTICLE 9 — SECURITY OF PERSON
  No person shall be deprived of life or liberty save in accordance with law.

ARTICLE 10 — SAFEGUARDS AS TO ARREST AND DETENTION
  (1) No person who is arrested shall be detained in custody without being
      informed of the grounds for such arrest.
  (2) Every person arrested and detained shall be produced before a magistrate
      within 24 hours of arrest.
  (3) No person shall be detained beyond the said period without authority
      of a magistrate.
  (4) No preventive detention shall exceed three months [as amended].

ARTICLE 10-A — RIGHT TO FAIR TRIAL
  [Inserted by 18th Amendment, 2010]
  For the determination of civil rights and obligations or in any criminal
  charge, every person shall be entitled to a fair trial and due process.

ARTICLE 11 — SLAVERY, FORCED LABOUR AND CHILD LABOUR PROHIBITED
  (1) Slavery is non-existent and forbidden; no law shall permit its introduction.
  (2) All forms of forced labour and traffic in human beings are prohibited.
  (3) No child below the age of fourteen years shall be engaged in any factory,
      mine, or any other hazardous employment.

ARTICLE 12 — PROTECTION AGAINST RETROSPECTIVE PUNISHMENT
  No person shall be punished for an act that was not an offence at the time
  of its commission, or by a penalty greater than prescribed at that time.

ARTICLE 13 — PROTECTION AGAINST DOUBLE PUNISHMENT AND SELF-INCRIMINATION
  No person shall be prosecuted or punished for the same offence more than
  once (Double Jeopardy), and no person shall be compelled to be a witness
  against himself.

ARTICLE 14 — INVIOLABILITY OF DIGNITY OF MAN
  The dignity of man and, subject to law, the privacy of home, shall be
  inviolable. No person shall be subjected to torture for the purpose
  of extracting evidence.

ARTICLE 15 — FREEDOM OF MOVEMENT
  Every citizen shall have the right to remain in, and move freely
  throughout, Pakistan, and to reside and settle in any part thereof.

ARTICLE 16 — FREEDOM OF ASSEMBLY
  Every citizen shall have the right to assemble peacefully and without
  arms, subject to reasonable restrictions in the interest of public order.

ARTICLE 17 — FREEDOM OF ASSOCIATION
  (1) Every citizen shall have the right to form associations or unions,
      subject to reasonable restrictions in the interest of sovereignty or
      integrity of Pakistan, public order or morality.
  (2) Every citizen, not being in the service of Pakistan, shall have the
      right to form or be a member of a political party, subject to law.
  (3) Every political party shall account for the source of its funds.
  (4) Every political party shall hold intra-party elections to elect its
      office-bearers and party leaders.

ARTICLE 18 — FREEDOM OF TRADE, BUSINESS OR PROFESSION
  Every citizen shall have the right to enter upon any lawful profession
  or occupation and to conduct any lawful trade or business.

ARTICLE 19 — FREEDOM OF SPEECH
  Every citizen shall have the right to freedom of speech and expression,
  and there shall be freedom of the press, subject to reasonable restrictions
  imposed by law in the interest of sovereignty and integrity of Pakistan,
  security of the State, friendly relations with foreign States, public order,
  decency or morality, or in relation to contempt of court or defamation.

ARTICLE 19-A — RIGHT TO INFORMATION
  [Inserted by 18th Amendment, 2010]
  Every citizen shall have the right to have access to information in all
  matters of public importance subject to regulation and reasonable
  restrictions imposed by law.

ARTICLE 20 — FREEDOM TO PROFESS RELIGION AND TO MANAGE RELIGIOUS INSTITUTIONS
  Subject to law, public order and morality:
    (a) Every citizen shall have the right to profess, practice and propagate
        his religion; and
    (b) Every religious denomination and every sect thereof shall have the
        right to establish, maintain and manage its religious institutions.

ARTICLE 21 — SAFEGUARD AGAINST TAXATION FOR PURPOSES OF ANY PARTICULAR RELIGION
  No person shall be compelled to pay any special tax, the proceeds of which
  are to be spent on the propagation or maintenance of any religion other
  than his own.

ARTICLE 22 — SAFEGUARDS AS TO EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTIONS
  No person attending any educational institution shall be required to receive
  religious instruction or take part in any religious ceremony relating to a
  religion other than his own.

ARTICLE 23 — PROVISION AS TO PROPERTY
  Every citizen shall have the right to acquire, hold and dispose of property
  in any part of Pakistan, subject to the Constitution and any reasonable
  restrictions imposed by law in the public interest.

ARTICLE 24 — PROTECTION OF PROPERTY RIGHTS
  No person shall be deprived of his property save in accordance with law.
  No property shall be compulsorily acquired or taken possession of save
  for a public purpose and with compensation.

ARTICLE 25 — EQUALITY OF CITIZENS
  (1) All citizens are equal before law and are entitled to equal protection of law.
  (2) There shall be no discrimination on the basis of sex alone.
  (3) Nothing prevents the State from making special provision for the
      protection of women and children.

ARTICLE 25-A — RIGHT TO EDUCATION
  [Inserted by 18th Amendment, 2010]
  The State shall provide free and compulsory education to all children
  of the age of five to sixteen years in such manner as may be determined
  by law.

ARTICLE 26 — NON-DISCRIMINATION IN ACCESS TO PUBLIC PLACES
  No discrimination against any citizen on the ground of race, religion,
  caste, sex, residence or place of birth in respect of access to places
  of public entertainment or resort not intended for religious purposes only.

ARTICLE 27 — SAFEGUARD AGAINST DISCRIMINATION IN SERVICES
  No citizen otherwise qualified for appointment in the service of Pakistan
  shall be discriminated against on grounds of race, religion, caste, sex,
  residence or place of birth.

ARTICLE 28 — PRESERVATION OF LANGUAGE, SCRIPT AND CULTURE
  Subject to Article 251, any section of citizens having a distinct language,
  script or culture shall have the right to preserve and promote the same.

PART III — PRINCIPLES OF POLICY (Articles 29–40)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 29 — PRINCIPLES OF POLICY
  It is the responsibility of each organ and authority of the State and
  of each person performing functions on behalf of an organ or authority
  of the State to act in accordance with the Principles of Policy.

ARTICLE 31 — ISLAMIC WAY OF LIFE
  Steps shall be taken to enable Muslims to order their lives in accordance
  with the fundamental principles and basic concepts of Islam.

ARTICLE 32 — PROMOTION OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT INSTITUTIONS
  The State shall encourage local Government institutions composed of
  elected representatives of the areas concerned.

ARTICLE 33 — PAROCHIALISM, RACIAL, TRIBAL, SECTARIAN AND PROVINCIAL PREJUDICES
  The State shall discourage parochial, racial, tribal, sectarian and
  provincial prejudices among citizens.

ARTICLE 34 — FULL PARTICIPATION OF WOMEN IN NATIONAL LIFE
  Steps shall be taken to ensure full participation of women in all spheres
  of national life.

ARTICLE 35 — PROTECTION OF FAMILY, ETC.
  The State shall protect the marriage, the family, the mother and the child.

ARTICLE 37 — PROMOTION OF SOCIAL JUSTICE AND ERADICATION OF SOCIAL EVILS
  The State shall provide basic necessities of life to citizens irrespective
  of sex, caste, creed or race who are unable to earn their livelihood.

ARTICLE 38 — PROMOTION OF SOCIAL AND ECONOMIC WELL-BEING
  The State shall secure the well-being of the people irrespective of sex,
  caste, creed or race by preventing concentration of wealth and means of
  production and distribution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART IV — THE FEDERATION OF PAKISTAN (Articles 41–100)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 41 — THE PRESIDENT
  There shall be a President of Pakistan who shall be the Head of State
  and shall represent the unity of the Republic.
  Qualifications: Muslim, citizen, not less than 45 years, qualified for
  election to the National Assembly.
  Term: 5 years. Elected by Electoral College (Parliament + Provincial Assemblies).

ARTICLE 46 — PRIME MINISTER TO KEEP PRESIDENT INFORMED
  The Prime Minister shall keep the President informed on all matters of
  internal and foreign policy and on all legislative proposals the Federal
  Government intends to bring before Parliament.

ARTICLE 50 — MAJLIS-E-SHOORA (PARLIAMENT)
  There shall be a Majlis-e-Shoora (Parliament) of Pakistan consisting of:
    (a) The President; and
    (b) Two Houses to be known as the National Assembly (Lower House) and
        the Senate (Upper House).

ARTICLE 51 — THE NATIONAL ASSEMBLY
  The National Assembly shall consist of 336 members (as per 25th Amendment).
  Directly elected from single member constituencies.
  Reserved seats for women and non-Muslims allocated by law.

ARTICLE 59 — THE SENATE
  The Senate shall consist of 96 members.
  Each Province shall have equal representation in the Senate.
  Senate represents federating units; cannot be dissolved.

ARTICLE 90 — FEDERAL GOVERNMENT
  The executive authority of the Federation shall be exercised in the name
  of the President by the Federal Government consisting of the Prime Minister
  and Federal Ministers.

ARTICLE 91 — THE CABINET
  There shall be a Cabinet of Ministers, with the Prime Minister at its head,
  to aid and advise the President in the exercise of his functions.
  The Prime Minister shall be a member of the National Assembly, a Muslim,
  of good character, not profligate, not convicted of moral turpitude.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART V — PARLIAMENT (Articles 101–174)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 111 — ADDRESS BY PRESIDENT
  The President may address either House or both Houses assembled together
  and for that purpose require the attendance of members.

ARTICLE 116 — ASSENT TO BILLS
  When a Bill has been passed by both Houses, it shall be presented to
  the President for assent.

ARTICLE 160 — NATIONAL FINANCE COMMISSION
  The President shall constitute a National Finance Commission consisting
  of the Minister of Finance of the Federal Government, Ministers of Finance
  of the Provincial Governments, and such other persons as the President
  may appoint. Commission shall make recommendations on distribution of
  revenues between Federation and Provinces.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART VI — JUDICIARY (Articles 175–212)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 175 — ESTABLISHMENT AND JURISDICTION OF COURTS
  (1) There shall be a Supreme Court of Pakistan.
  (2) There shall be a High Court for each Province.
  (3) The judiciary shall be separated from the executive progressively.

ARTICLE 176 — CONSTITUTION OF SUPREME COURT
  The Supreme Court shall consist of a Chief Justice to be known as the
  Chief Justice of Pakistan, and such other Judges as may be determined
  by Act of Parliament.

ARTICLE 184 — ORIGINAL JURISDICTION OF SUPREME COURT
  The Supreme Court shall have original jurisdiction to enforce fundamental
  rights if it considers a question of public importance is involved.
  [Suo Motu powers vested under this Article.]

ARTICLE 185 — APPELLATE JURISDICTION OF SUPREME COURT
  The Supreme Court shall have jurisdiction to hear and determine appeals
  from any judgment, decree, order or sentence of a High Court.

ARTICLE 199 — JURISDICTION OF HIGH COURT
  A High Court may issue to any person or authority, including the Government:
    (a) A direction ordering the performance of any specific duty;
    (b) A writ of Habeas Corpus;
    (c) A writ of Mandamus;
    (d) A writ of Certiorari;
    (e) A writ of Quo Warranto; and
    (f) A writ of Prohibition.

ARTICLE 203-A TO 203-J — FEDERAL SHARIAT COURT
  There shall be a Federal Shariat Court consisting of not more than eight
  Muslim Judges including the Chief Justice.
  The Court shall examine and decide whether any law is repugnant to the
  injunctions of Islam as laid down in the Holy Quran and Sunnah.

ARTICLE 209 — SUPREME JUDICIAL COUNCIL
  There shall be a Supreme Judicial Council of Pakistan consisting of:
    - Chief Justice of Pakistan (Chairman)
    - Two next most senior Judges of the Supreme Court
    - Chief Justices of the two most senior High Courts
  The Council investigates misconduct of judges of the Supreme Court
  and High Courts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART VII — ELECTIONS (Articles 213–226)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 213 — ELECTION COMMISSION OF PAKISTAN
  There shall be an Election Commission of Pakistan consisting of:
    - A Chairman (Chief Election Commissioner), and
    - Four Members, one from each Province.
  The Chief Election Commissioner shall be a person who has been a Judge
  of the Supreme Court.

ARTICLE 218 — CONDUCT OF ELECTIONS
  The Election Commission shall be responsible for organizing and conducting
  elections to the National Assembly, Senate and Provincial Assemblies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART VIII — ISLAMIC PROVISIONS (Articles 227–231)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 227 — PROVISIONS RELATING TO THE QURAN AND SUNNAH
  All existing laws shall be brought in conformity with the Injunctions
  of Islam as laid down in the Holy Quran and Sunnah, and no law shall
  be enacted which is repugnant to such Injunctions.

ARTICLE 228 — COUNCIL OF ISLAMIC IDEOLOGY
  There shall be a Council of Islamic Ideology consisting of not more than
  twenty members, including at least one woman, appointed by the President
  after consultation with the Prime Minister.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART IX — EMERGENCY PROVISIONS (Articles 232–237)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 232 — PROCLAMATION OF EMERGENCY
  If the President is satisfied that a grave emergency exists in which the
  security or economic life of Pakistan, or any part thereof, is threatened
  by war or external aggression or by internal disturbance beyond the power
  of a Provincial Government to control, he may issue a Proclamation of
  Emergency.

ARTICLE 234 — EMERGENCY POWERS TO FEDERATION
  While a Proclamation of Emergency is in operation relating to a Province,
  the executive authority of the Federation shall extend to giving of
  directions to the Province as to the manner in which its executive
  authority is to be exercised.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART XII — MISCELLANEOUS (Articles 255–280)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARTICLE 260 — DEFINITIONS
  "The State" includes: Federal Government, Majlis-e-Shoora (Parliament),
  Provincial Governments, Provincial Assemblies and Local Authorities.

ARTICLE 268 — CONTINUATION OF EXISTING LAWS
  All laws in force immediately before the commencing day shall continue
  in force subject to the Constitution.

ARTICLE 270-AA — VALIDATION OF LAWS, ETC.
  Relates to the validation of laws made during certain periods.

A. PAKISTAN PENAL CODE, 1860 (PPC)
   [Act XLV of 1860 | 6 October 1860 | As amended to date]
   [23 Chapters | 511 Sections | Jurisdiction: Whole of Pakistan]
   [Source: pakistancode.gov.pk | pakistani.org]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

PREAMBLE: Whereas it is expedient to provide a general Penal Code for Pakistan.

CHAPTER I — INTRODUCTION
  Section 1 — Title and Extent: This Act shall be called the Pakistan Penal
    Code and shall take effect throughout Pakistan.
  Section 2 — Punishment within Pakistan: Every person shall be liable to
    punishment for every act or omission contrary to provisions hereof
    of which he shall be guilty within Pakistan.
  Section 3 — Extra-territorial Offences: Covers persons who commit offences
    beyond Pakistan but which may be tried within Pakistan by law.
  Section 4 — Extension to Extra-territorial Offences: Covers offences
    committed on ships or aircraft registered in Pakistan.
  Section 5 — Certain Laws Not Affected: The Code does not affect Indian
    Articles of War (applicable to armed forces) or court martial laws.

CHAPTER II — GENERAL EXPLANATIONS (Sections 6–52A)
  Section 6 — Definitions subject to exceptions and provisions.
  Section 8 — "Gender" — masculine gender imports the feminine also.
  Section 10 — "Man" includes male of any age; "Woman" any female of any age.
  Section 11 — "Person" includes company, association or body of persons.
  Section 21 — "Public Servant" — includes judges, police, government servants.
  Section 34 — ACTS DONE BY SEVERAL PERSONS IN FURTHERANCE OF COMMON INTENTION:
    When a criminal act is done by several persons in furtherance of the
    common intention of all, each of such persons is liable for that act
    in the same manner as if it were done by him alone.

CHAPTER III — PUNISHMENTS (Sections 53–75)
  Section 53 — Punishments: The punishments to which offenders are liable:
    (a) Death;
    (b) Imprisonment for life;
    (c) Rigorous imprisonment (with hard labour);
    (d) Simple imprisonment;
    (e) Forfeiture of property;
    (f) Fine;
    (g) Hadd (as prescribed by Islamic law);
    (h) Tazir (discretionary punishment);
    (i) Whipping (where prescribed under special laws).
  Section 54 — Commutation of sentence of death.
  Section 57 — Fractions of terms of punishment.
  Section 63 — Fine — where term in default of fine is not expressed.
  Section 71 — Limit of punishment of offence made up of several offences.
  Section 73 — Solitary confinement.
  Section 75 — Enhanced punishment for certain offences after previous conviction.

CHAPTER IV — GENERAL EXCEPTIONS (Sections 76–106)
  Section 76 — Act done by a person bound by law.
  Section 77 — Act of judge when acting judicially.
  Section 78 — Act done pursuant to order or judgment of Court.
  Section 79 — Act done by a person justified by law.
  Section 80 — Accident in doing a lawful act.
  Section 81 — Act likely to cause harm but done without criminal intent.
  Section 82 — Act of a child under seven years of age — No offence.
  Section 83 — Act of child above 7 and under 12 of immature understanding.
  Section 84 — Act of a person of unsound mind — Not an offence.
  Section 85 — Act of a person incapable due to intoxication against his will.
  Section 96 — Things done in private defence — Not an offence.
  Section 100 — When the right of private defence of the body extends to
    causing death: Assault reasonably causing apprehension of death, grievous
    hurt, rape, kidnapping, abduction, or acid attack.
  Section 105 — Commencement and continuation of the right of private defence
    of property.

CHAPTER VI — OFFENCES AGAINST THE STATE (Sections 121–130)
  Section 121 — Waging war against Pakistan: Punishment — DEATH or
    imprisonment for life, plus fine.
  Section 121-A — Conspiracy to commit offences under Section 121:
    Punishment — Death or imprisonment for life or up to 10 years, plus fine.
  Section 122 — Collecting arms with intention of waging war: Imprisonment
    for life or up to 10 years, plus fine.
  Section 123 — Concealment of design to wage war: Imprisonment up to 10 years
    plus fine.
  Section 123-A — Condemning the creation of Pakistan: Imprisonment up to 10
    years and fine. [Inserted 1950]
  Section 124-A — Sedition: Whoever by words, spoken or written, or signs, or
    visible representation, excites or attempts to excite disaffection towards
    the Government shall be punished with imprisonment for life or for 3 years.

CHAPTER VII — OFFENCES RELATING TO THE ARMY, NAVY AND AIR FORCE
  Section 131 — Abetting mutiny, attempt to seduce soldier or sailor from duty:
    Death or imprisonment for life.

CHAPTER VIII — OFFENCES AGAINST PUBLIC TRANQUILLITY
  Section 141 — UNLAWFUL ASSEMBLY: An assembly of five or more persons is
    "unlawful assembly" if the common object is to:
    - Overawe Government or Legislature by criminal force or show thereof;
    - Resist execution of legal process;
    - Commit mischief or criminal trespass;
    - Obtain by force possession of property.
  Section 143 — Punishment for unlawful assembly: Up to 6 months or fine or both.
  Section 144 — Joining assembly with deadly weapon: Up to 2 years or fine or both.
  Section 147 — Rioting: Up to 2 years or fine or both.
  Section 148 — Rioting armed with deadly weapon: Up to 3 years or fine or both.
  Section 149 — Every member of unlawful assembly guilty of offence committed
    in prosecution of common object.

CHAPTER IX — OFFENCES BY OR RELATING TO PUBLIC SERVANTS
  Section 161 — Public servant taking gratification other than legal remuneration
    in respect of official act: Up to 3 years or fine or both.
  Section 166 — Public servant disobeying law with intent to cause injury: Up
    to 1 year or fine or both.
  Section 167 — Public servant framing incorrect document: Up to 3 years or fine.
  Section 168 — Public servant unlawfully engaging in trade: Up to 1 year or fine.

CHAPTER X — CONTEMPT OF LAWFUL AUTHORITY OF PUBLIC SERVANTS
  Section 172 — Absconding to avoid service of summons: Fine up to 500 rupees.
  Section 174 — Non-attendance in obedience to summons: Fine up to 500 rupees.
  Section 175 — Omission to produce document: Fine up to 1000 rupees.
  Section 179 — Refusing to answer public servant authorised to question:
    Up to 6 months or fine.
  Section 182 — False information to public servant: Up to 6 months or fine.
  Section 186 — Obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions:
    Up to 3 months or fine up to 500 rupees or both.
  Section 189 — Threat of injury to public servant: Up to 2 years or fine.

CHAPTER XI — FALSE EVIDENCE AND OFFENCES AGAINST PUBLIC JUSTICE
  Section 191 — Giving false evidence (Perjury): Punishable under Section 193.
  Section 193 — Punishment for false evidence: Up to 7 years and fine.
  Section 194 — Giving or fabricating false evidence with intent to procure
    conviction of capital offence: Death or imprisonment for life or up to
    10 years plus fine.
  Section 201 — Causing disappearance of evidence of offence or giving false
    information to screen offender.
  Section 202 — Intentional omission to give information of offence by
    person bound to inform.
  Section 204 — Destruction of document to prevent its production as evidence.

CHAPTER XII — OFFENCES RELATING TO COIN AND GOVERNMENT STAMPS
  Section 231 — Counterfeiting coin: Up to 7 years and fine.
  Section 232 — Counterfeiting Pakistani coin: Up to 10 years and fine.

CHAPTER XIV — OFFENCES AFFECTING PUBLIC HEALTH, SAFETY, CONVENIENCE
  Section 268 — Public nuisance defined.
  Section 269 — Negligent act likely to spread infection: Up to 6 months or fine.
  Section 272 — Adulteration of food or drink intended for sale: Up to 6 months
    or fine or both.
  Section 279 — Rash driving or riding on a public way: Up to 6 months or fine.

  Section 295 — Injuring or defiling place of worship: Up to 2 years or fine.

    Up to 10 years or fine or both.
  Section 295-B — Defiling copy of Holy Quran:

  Section 295-C — Use of derogatory remarks in respect of the Holy Prophet (PBUH):

CHAPTER XVI — OFFENCES AFFECTING THE HUMAN BODY (Sections 299–377)

OFFENCES RELATING TO HUMAN BODY:

  Section 299 — Definitions:
    "Amd" — intentional; "Shibh-i-Amd" — intentional without intention to kill;
    "Khata" — mistake; "Hadd" — punishment ordained by Holy Quran;
    "Tazir" — punishment at discretion of court; "Diyat" — compensation
    paid to heirs of victim; "Arsh" — compensation for bodily hurt.

  Section 300 — QATL-I-AMAL (MURDER): Whoever causes death of a person
    commits Qatl-i-Amd.
  Section 302 — Punishment for Qatl-i-Amd (Murder):
    (a) Qisas — death as Hadd;
    (b) If waived by heirs — Diyat; or
    (c) Tazir — death or imprisonment for life where proof in Hadd not
        available but act proved beyond doubt.
  Section 306 — Qatl Shibh-i-Amd (intentional but without intent to kill):
    Diyat and Tazir.
  Section 320 — Grievous Hurt: Causing hurt to the extent specified.
  Section 322 — Qatl-i-Khata (culpable homicide by mistake): Diyat.
  Section 325 — Punishment for Ghayar-i-Jaifah (non-penetrating hurt):
    Arsh (specified compensation).

OFFENCES RELATING TO RAPE (As amended by Criminal Law Amendment Acts 2016 & 2021):
  Section 375 — RAPE: A man is said to commit rape who has sexual intercourse

    in fear of death, or when she is unable to give consent.
    [Includes gang rape; child rape; rape by armed forces members in custody]
  Section 376 — Punishment for Rape:
    - Simple rape: Death or imprisonment not less than 10 years but up to 25 years.
    - Gang rape: Death or imprisonment for life (each offender).
    - Rape of child: Death or imprisonment not less than 25 years.
    [Offender may be chemically castrated per discretion of court — 2021 Amendment]

OFFENCES RELATING TO KIDNAPPING AND ABDUCTION:
  Section 359 — Kidnapping: From Pakistan, or from lawful guardianship.
  Section 363 — Punishment for kidnapping: Up to 7 years and fine.

  Section 364-A — Kidnapping for ransom: DEATH or imprisonment for life.
  Section 365 — Kidnapping or abducting with intent to secretly and wrongfully

  Section 365-A — Kidnapping for extortion: Death or imprisonment for life.

  Section 369 — Kidnapping or abducting child under 10 for taking property:

  Section 415 — CHEATING: Whoever by deceiving any person, fraudulently or

  Section 421 — Dishonest or fraudulent removal or concealment of property.

  Section 436 — Mischief by fire (house, etc.): Imprisonment for life or

  Section 441 — CRIMINAL TRESPASS: Entering on property of another with

B. CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE, 1898 (Cr.P.C.)

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

  Section 32 — Sentence of Magistrate of First Class: Up to 5 years rigorous or

  Section 154 — FIR (FIRST INFORMATION REPORT): Every information relating to
    commission of a cognizable offence, if given orally to an officer in charge

  Section 155 — Information as to non-cognizable cases: Police shall record

  Section 156 — Police officer's power to investigate cognizable case: Officer

  Section 157 — Procedure where cognizable offence suspected.
  Section 161 — Examination of witnesses by police.
  Section 162 — Statements to police not to be signed; use of statements.
  Section 164 — Power to record statements and confessions [before Magistrate].
  Section 167 — Procedure when investigation cannot be completed in 24 hours:

  Section 173 — Report of police officer: Police must send report upon completion

CHAPTER XV — BAIL (Sections 496–502)
  Section 496 — Bail in bailable offences: When any person accused of bailable
    offence is arrested or detained, he shall be released on bail.
  Section 497 — Bail in non-bailable offences: Person accused of non-bailable
    offence may be released on bail at discretion of court or police officer.
    Proviso: No person accused of non-bailable offence punishable with death
    or imprisonment for life shall be released on bail unless special reasons.
  Section 498 — Power of High Court and Court of Session to grant bail in
    non-bailable cases; Anticipatory bail (pre-arrest bail).

  Section 265-A — SESSIONS TRIAL: Application of Chapter XXIII.
  Section 265-D — Framing of charge: Court frames charge against accused.
  Section 265-E — Conviction on plea of guilty.
  Section 265-F — Evidence for prosecution.
  Section 265-G — Acquittal: If after prosecution evidence, no case established.
  Section 265-H — Defence: Accused may adduce evidence in defence.
  Section 265-K — Judgment: Court pronounces judgment.

  Section 382-B — Period of detention counted towards sentence: Period during

  Section 410 — Appeals from convictions: Lies to High Court in cases of Sessions.

D. NATIONAL ACCOUNTABILITY ORDINANCE, 1999 (NAO / NAB Ordinance)
   [Ordinance XVIII of 1999 | 16 November 1999]
   [As amended by NAB Amendment Acts 2022 (restored by Supreme Court 2024)]
   [Source: sbp.org.pk | pakistani.org | sindhhighcourt.gov.pk]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

PREAMBLE: To provide for setting up a National Accountability Bureau to eradicate
corruption and corrupt practices and hold accountable all persons accused of
such practices and matters ancillary thereto.

  Section 2 — Definitions:
    "Corruption" — includes taking gratification as motive or reward for doing
      or forbearing to do any official act; or misuse of power; or having assets
      beyond known sources of income (unexplained assets);
    "Holder of Public Office" — includes President, Prime Minister, Ministers,
      Governors, Members of Parliament, judges, civil servants;
    [Note: Superior court judges and armed forces personnel are excluded
     per constitutional provisions — Art. 209 (SJC) and Army Act 1952.]

  Section 4 — Application of Ordinance: Applies to all public office holders
    and other persons who commit corruption and corrupt practices.

  Section 9 — Corruption and corrupt practices: A holder of public office
    or any other person is said to be guilty if he:
    (a) Accepts or obtains any gratification in contravention of law;
    (b) Accepts any valuable thing without adequate consideration;
    (c) Dishonestly or fraudulently misappropriates or embezzles property;
    (d) By corrupt or illegal means obtains from a public servant any public
        property;
    (e) Has or had assets or pecuniary resources disproportionate to known
        sources of income (Reverse burden of proof on accused).

  Section 10 — Punishment for corruption and corrupt practices:
    - Imprisonment up to 14 years;
    - Fine;
    - Disqualification from holding public office.

  Section 12 — Freezing and attachment of assets by Chairman NAB.
  Section 14 — Voluntary Return / Plea Bargain: Amount of looted money
    may be recovered without prosecution if accepted by Chairman NAB.
  Section 15 — ARREST: Chairman NAB may authorise arrest.
  Section 16 — Reference before Accountability Court by NAB.
  Section 21 — Jurisdiction of Accountability Courts: Special courts.
  Section 25 — Onus of proof: Where assets are disproportionate to income,
    onus of proof lies on the accused to explain the source.
  Section 32 — Provisions of Cr.P.C. apply to extent not inconsistent.
  Section 33 — Overriding effect: Ordinance has effect notwithstanding
    anything in any other law.

  [2022 AMENDMENTS (Restored by Supreme Court 5-0, September 2024)]:
  - NAB's jurisdiction limited; Federal cabinet decisions exempted from NAB inquiry.
  - Threshold for "corruption" raised: cases below PKR 500 million referred
    to relevant authorities.
  - Definition of "public office" refined.
  - Bail conditions eased (accused may not be detained beyond 3 months).

A. JUDICIARY

SUPREME COURT OF PAKISTAN:
  Constitutional Basis: Articles 176–191 of the Constitution.
  Composition: Chief Justice of Pakistan + Judges (minimum 16 per statute).
  Jurisdiction:
    - Original: Article 184(3) — enforcement of fundamental rights.
    - Appellate: Article 185 — from High Courts.
    - Advisory: Article 186 — Federal Government may seek opinion.
    - Review: Supreme Court may review its own judgments.
  Suo Motu: Court may take cognizance of its own motion on matters of public
    importance under Article 184(3).
  Practice and Procedure: Supreme Court (Practice and Procedure) Act, 2023
    [Enacted to regulate constitution of benches; bench formation by committee].
  26th Amendment (2024): Constitutional Benches created within Supreme Court
    for specified constitutional matters.
  27th Amendment (2025): Federal Constitutional Court introduced as separate
    court; jurisdiction over specific constitutional questions.

FEDERAL SHARIAT COURT:
  Constitutional Basis: Articles 203A–203J.
  Jurisdiction: Examines any law and decides if repugnant to Islam.
  Composition: Maximum 8 Muslim Judges including Chief Justice.
  No jurisdiction over Constitution, Muslim Personal Law, Fiscal Laws, or
  procedural laws (for 3 years initially — now expired limitation).

HIGH COURTS:
  Constitutional Basis: Articles 192–203.
  One High Court for each Province:
    - Lahore High Court (Punjab);
    - High Court of Sindh (Sindh);
    - Peshawar High Court (KPK);
    - Balochistan High Court (Balochistan);
    - Islamabad High Court (ICT) — established 2010.
  Writ jurisdiction under Article 199.
  Appellate jurisdiction over subordinate courts.

DISTRICT COURTS:
  Statutory basis under Cr.P.C. and Civil Procedure Code, 1908.
  Sessions Courts — criminal cases.
  District Courts — civil matters.
  Family Courts — under West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964.

SUPREME JUDICIAL COUNCIL:
  Constitutional Basis: Article 209.
  Investigates misconduct of Supreme Court and High Court judges.
  Reference can be made by President or on complaint.
  May recommend removal of judge to President.

APPOINTMENT OF JUDGES (26th Amendment 2024):
  Judicial Commission of Pakistan recommends nominees.
  Parliamentary Committee scrutinises and confirms appointments.
  Chief Justice appointed by President on recommendation of Judicial Commission.

A. MUSLIM FAMILY LAWS ORDINANCE, 1961 (MFLO)
   [Ordinance VIII of 1961 | Applies to all Muslims in Pakistan]
   [As amended, including Punjab Muslim Family Laws (Amendment) Act 2015]
   [Source: punjablaws.gov.pk | pakistancode.gov.pk | citylaws.pk]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

PREAMBLE: To give effect to certain recommendations of the Commission on
Marriage and Family Laws.

  Section 1 — Short title, extent and application:
    Applies to all Muslim citizens of Pakistan, wherever they may be.
    Extends to the whole of Pakistan [as amended by various provincial acts].

  Section 3 — Ordinance to override other laws:
    Provisions of this Ordinance shall have effect notwithstanding any law,
    custom or usage. Registration of Muslim marriages shall take place only
    in accordance with these provisions.
    Arbitration Act and CPC shall not apply to any Arbitration Council.

  Section 4 — SUCCESSION:
    In the event of the death of any son or daughter of the propositus
    before the opening of succession, the children of such son or daughter,
    if living at the time succession opens, shall per stirpes receive a
    share equivalent to the share such son or daughter would have received
    if alive. [This rule modifies the strict classical Islamic inheritance rules.]

  Section 5 — REGISTRATION OF MARRIAGES:
    (1) Every marriage solemnised under Muslim Law shall be registered in
        accordance with provisions of this Ordinance.
    (2) The Nikah Registrar (Union Council area) shall register the marriage.
    (3) Failure to register: Shall not affect validity of marriage; but
        person responsible shall be punished with simple imprisonment up to
        3 months or fine up to PKR 1,000 or both.

  Section 6 — POLYGAMY (Additional Marriages):
    (1) No man during the subsistence of an existing marriage shall contract
        another marriage except with previous permission in writing of the
        Arbitration Council.
    (2) Application to Arbitration Council: Must state grounds of necessity;
        Council shall examine and decide whether proposed marriage is
        necessary and just.
    (3) Permission shall be granted if Arbitration Council satisfied marriage
        is necessary and just.
    (4) Contravention: Offender shall pay immediate dower (Mehr) of existing
        wife; and shall be punished with simple imprisonment up to 1 year or
        fine up to PKR 5,000 or both.
    (5) Existing wife may apply to divorce upon husband contracting second
        marriage without permission.

  Section 7 — TALAQ (DIVORCE BY HUSBAND):
    (1) Any man who wishes to divorce his wife shall, as soon as may be after
        pronouncement of Talaq in any form whatsoever, give written notice to
        Chairman, Arbitration Council, and supply copy to wife.
    (2) Failure to give notice: Punishable with simple imprisonment up to 1 year
        or fine up to PKR 5,000 or both; and divorce shall not be effective.
    (3) Arbitration Council shall constitute an Arbitration Council within 30
        days and try to bring about reconciliation.
    (4) Talaq shall not be effective until 90 days from service of notice on
        Chairman have elapsed; if wife is pregnant, not until end of pregnancy.
    (5) If reconciliation takes place, Talaq shall not take effect. Husband may
        revoke the Talaq.

  Section 8 — DELEGATION OF RIGHT OF DIVORCE (TALAQ-E-TAFWEEZ):
    A wife may exercise the right of Talaq delegated to her by husband
    by pronouncing Talaq herself. The procedure of Section 7 shall apply.

  Section 9 — MAINTENANCE:
    (1) If husband fails to maintain wife adequately or there is disagreement
        as to the amount of maintenance, wife or husband may apply to Arbitration
        Council for determining maintenance.
    (2) Arbitration Council shall determine maintenance after hearing parties.
    (3) Certificate of Arbitration Council for maintenance shall be recoverable
        as arrears of land revenue.

  Section 10 — DOWER (MEHR):
    Where no details of dower are given at time of marriage, dower shall be
    payable on demand. Dower shall be recoverable as arrears of land revenue.

B. DISSOLUTION OF MUSLIM MARRIAGES ACT, 1939 (DMMA)
   [Act VIII of 1939 | Applies to Muslim women]
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Section 2 — GROUNDS FOR KHULA (DIVORCE BY WIFE):
    A woman married under Muslim Law shall be entitled to obtain a decree for
    dissolution of her marriage (Khula) on any one or more of the following
    grounds:
    (i) Whereabouts of husband unknown for 4 years;
    (ii) Failure to maintain for 2 years;
    (iii) Husband sentenced to imprisonment for 7 or more years;
    (iv) Failure to perform marital obligations without reasonable cause for
         3 years;
    (v) Husband was impotent at time of marriage;
    (vi) Husband was insane for 2 years or suffers from leprosy or virulent
         venereal disease;
    (vii) Wife given in marriage by father or guardian before age of 15 —
          can repudiate before 18 (Dissolution of marriage by Minor);
    (viii) Husband treats wife with cruelty — habitual assault or making her
           life miserable; associates with women of ill repute; attempts to
           force wife to lead immoral life; disposes of wife's property;
           obstructs wife's religious practice; does not treat co-wives equally;
    (ix) Any other ground recognized as valid dissolution under Muslim Law
         (including Khula on wife's offer to return Mehr).

I. INHERITANCE LAW (Islamic Law / Shariah for Muslims)
------------------------------------------------------------------------

  SOURCE: Holy Quran (Surah An-Nisa: 4:11-12, 4:176) and Hadith;
    Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1962.

  GENERAL RULES OF INHERITANCE UNDER ISLAMIC LAW:

  Male heirs generally receive double the share of female heirs:
    - Son: Double share of daughter.
    - Father: Entitled when no son.
    - Husband: 1/4 of wife's estate if they have children; 1/2 if no children.
    - Wife: 1/8 of husband's estate if there are children; 1/4 if no children.
    - Daughter: 1/2 if only child; 2/3 if more than one daughter.
    - Mother: 1/6 of estate if there are children or brothers/sisters.
    - Sister: In absence of other heirs, various shares apply.

  CONDITION FOR INHERITANCE:
    - Heir must be alive at time of succession.
    - Heir must not have murdered the deceased.
    - Non-Muslims cannot inherit from Muslims (general rule).

  WASIYAT (WILL / BEQUEST):
    - A Muslim can bequeath up to 1/3 of his estate by will.
    - Bequest beyond 1/3 requires consent of heirs.
    - A bequest to an heir is not valid without consent of other heirs.

  [Key Constitutional Provision: Section 498-A PPC (2011) — Criminal
   offense to deprive women from inheriting property by use of force or fraud.]

  [Key Case: Hafeez-ur-Rehman v. Saif-ur-Rehman PLD 2017 SC 249 — Supreme
   Court reinforced that women cannot be deprived of inheritance rights.]

  WEST PAKISTAN MUSLIM PERSONAL LAW (SHARIAT) APPLICATION ACT, 1962:
    Applied Islamic law on succession, inheritance, and related matters
    as the rule of decision in courts of Pakistan for Muslims.
